Project overview

Stop guessing which exercise works best.

Muscle activity is usually invisible. Beginners often rely on feeling or guesswork to know whether an exercise is working.

The solution

Turn muscle signals into useful feedback

MyEMG turns muscle signals into a live 0–100% activation level. Users can perform different exercises for the same target muscle and compare Average and Peak activation in the app. The current app supports Biceps, Triceps and Legs, and can be extended to other target muscles.

Cheez.sEMG sensor XIAO ESP32C3 BLE wireless Flutter app
How it works

Four steps. One live signal.

01

Choose a target muscle

Select the muscle you want to train or test.

02

Wear and calibrate

Place the EMG sensor on the target muscle, then follow a short guided calibration flow.

03

Watch live activation

See muscle effort as a real-time percentage and vertical bar chart in the app.

04

Compare exercises

MyEMG saves Average, Peak, Time and Reps, then ranks which movement activated the target muscle most effectively.

Device / prototype

Designed to move with you.

The wearable prototype combines a Cheez.sEMG sensor, XIAO ESP32C3 board, battery power and a 3D-printed case designed to be attached to the arm.

  • Wearable target-muscle placement
  • Compact embedded hardware
  • Wireless, untethered feedback

App preview

Live feedback that leads to a clearer choice.

The app shows live activation through a percentage and vertical bar chart. After training, each session is saved with Average, Peak, Time and Reps. Best Exercises ranks saved exercises for the selected muscle, helping users understand which movement worked better. The example may show Biceps, but the same concept applies to any selected target muscle.

Technical stack

Built across hardware and software.

Hardware

Cheez.sEMG sensor, XIAO ESP32C3, battery-powered wearable prototype and 3D-printed case.

Firmware

Arduino C++, ADC sampling, envelope processing, calibration routine and BLE notify/write.

App

Flutter, Dart, BLE dashboard, raw waveform view, session tracking and exercise ranking.
